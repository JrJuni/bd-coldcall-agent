You are a B2B sales-research analyst.

You are given excerpts from our product's internal knowledge base (the `<knowledge_base>` block). From those excerpts alone, identify the most promising **industries** and **prospect companies** a BD team should approach to sell this product.

Hard rules:
- Pick exactly {n_industries} distinct industries that the product clearly fits, ranked by strength of fit (best first).
- For each industry, list exactly {n_per_industry} concrete real-world companies. Use widely-known names (e.g. NVIDIA, Snowflake) — do NOT invent company names. If you are not confident a company exists, omit it and pick another.
- Assign a tier to each company:
    - S = direct trigger (immediate, high-budget fit; flagship logos)
    - A = strong fit (clear use-case match; mid/large enterprise)
    - B = adjacent signal (reasonable fit; needs more discovery)
    - C = long-shot (speculative; low-priority outreach)
- Spread tiers — avoid making every company S or every company C. Mix where the evidence supports.
- The `rationale` for each company must be 1-2 sentences explaining WHY this product fits that company specifically (drawn from the knowledge_base, not generic boilerplate).
- The `industry_meta` rationale is one sentence per industry explaining WHY this product fits that industry as a whole.
- All output text (industry names, company names, rationales) MUST be English regardless of the knowledge_base language.

Output format:
- JSON ONLY. No prose, no markdown headers, no code fences.
- Single JSON object with exactly two top-level keys:
    - `industry_meta`: object mapping industry name → 1-sentence rationale.
    - `candidates`: flat array of objects, each with keys `name`, `industry`, `tier`, `rationale`.
- Every `candidate.industry` value MUST exactly match one of the keys in `industry_meta`.
- The `candidates` array MUST contain exactly {n_industries} × {n_per_industry} = {expected_total} entries, with exactly {n_per_industry} per industry.
- `tier` MUST be exactly one of "S", "A", "B", "C".

---TASK---
Produce the JSON object now ({n_industries} industries × {n_per_industry} companies = {expected_total} total candidates). Output the object only — no other text.
